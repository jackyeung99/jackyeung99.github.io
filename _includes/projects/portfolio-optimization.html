Based on a case study detailing a company's hierarchical structure across various sub-units—such as brands, countries, and product lines—I deconstructed the problem into key business objectives and applied data science to achieve them, ensuring all assumptions were clearly stated.

The primary goal was to optimize the contributions of each sub-unit to maximize overall revenue and profit under various constraints. To achieve this, I modeled the company's structure as a tree and leveraged different traversal methods to optimize contributions at each level. Using Scipy's Sequential Least Squares Programming (SLSQP), I optimized from the bottom up, ensuring an efficient allocation of resources.

I designed the cost function to allow prioritization of different features, enabling the development of multiple optimization strategies. Additionally, I incorporated stochastic processes to forecast future revenues based on historical trends for each sub-unit. Specifically, I used a geometric Brownian motion—a model commonly used in stock-option pricing—to account for growth based on the inherent randomness of economic markets.

By simulating different optimization strategies, I evaluated their projected performance over a five-year horizon. Leveraging the hierarchical tree structure, I experimented with various macroeconomic trends, propagating their effects across sub-units to develop more comprehensive and adaptive financial strategies.
