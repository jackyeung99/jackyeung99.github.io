I trained multiple classification algorithms, including Support Vector Machine (SVM), Random Forest, and Logistic Regression, to predict fraudulent credit card transactions of European card holders. To enhance predictive performance, I combined these models using ensemble methods.

While all of the models performed well, the ensemble soft voting performed the best achieving a 98% accuracy in fraud detection. The predictions were based on 14 anonymized features.

It is important to note that the features in the dataset were strong predictors, and such high accuracy may not be achievable when working with the challenges of real-world data. However, this assignment provided me with hands-on experience in using ensemble methods, which could be valuable in practical applications.

Dataset

The dataset used for this project was sourced from Kaggle: Credit Card Fraud Detection Dataset (2023) .
